SAT vs AP: Which to Prioritise for US Applications? (2026 Update)

By DAYA Elite Tutoring · International curricula & admissions tutors · Updated 2026

"My child is applying to US universities — should we prepare the SAT or AP first?" It's a common dilemma for Chinese parents. In reality it isn't either/or — they do different jobs in an application. This guide explains what each is for, one key 2026 policy change (the SAT is required again at most top US universities), and how to prioritise along the timeline.

In this guide
  • What the SAT and AP each do
  • The key 2026 change: the SAT is required again
  • How to prioritise (by timeline)
  • Where Chinese students usually lose marks

1. What the SAT and AP each do

SATAP
What it isThe standardised US admissions test, out of 1600 (now a digital, adaptive exam)Subject-by-subject exams, scored 1-5, showing depth in a subject
RoleA comparable hard metric almost every applicant submitsDemonstrates academic rigour; high scores can earn college credit
WhenUsually junior year, can be retaken for a higher scoreEach May, taken per course studied

In one line: the SAT is the ticket, AP is the proof of depth. They complement each other — they don't compete.

2. The key 2026 change: the SAT is required again

During the pandemic many top schools went "test-optional". But for the 2026-27 cycle, most top US universities have reinstated the SAT/ACT as required — including Harvard, Yale, Brown, Cornell, Dartmouth, MIT, Caltech and Stanford; Columbia remains test-optional. Policies change every year, so always check each university's current requirement — but for international applicants the practical stance now is: prepare for the SAT as a default requirement.

What this means for parents: families still betting on "we won't submit a score" now carry more risk. Treating the SAT as required and planning early is the safer call.

3. How to prioritise (by timeline)

  1. Lock in a competitive SAT score first. It's the hard metric nearly every school sees and it can be retaken — secure it early and the rest is calmer.
  2. Pick APs that match the intended major. Aiming at STEM → Calculus, Physics, Chemistry; business → Economics, Statistics. AP shows the student is serious in their target field.
  3. Don't overload. A few APs scored 5 beat a pile scored 3. Quality first.
  4. Stagger the peaks. The SAT can be scheduled flexibly; AP is fixed each May — keep the two prep peaks from colliding.

4. Where Chinese students usually lose marks

Chinese students in Singapore and Malaysia are typically strong at maths, so SAT Maths and science APs are usually fine. The real loss is in English — the SAT's Reading & Writing section is all in English and fast-paced, and AP written responses demand solid English too. Targeted English exam technique is usually the fastest way to lift the total.
